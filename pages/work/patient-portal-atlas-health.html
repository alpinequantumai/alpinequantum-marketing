Client Story · Healthcare · 22 weeks · 3 phases

Patient portal modernization across 2.8M annual visits.

A multi-specialty healthcare network consolidated three disconnected patient-facing systems into a single modern portal — with FHIR-native EHR integration, AI-powered triage, and a 52% reduction in onboarding time.

Client
Atlas Health Network
Industry
Healthcare
Services
Software Engineering, AI, HIPAA Cloud
Timeline
22 weeks · 3 phases
The challenge

Three portals, zero alignment.

Atlas Health Network serves 2.8 million patient visits annually across 14 specialty practices and 23 facilities. Through years of acquisitions and platform shifts, patient experience had fragmented across three separate portals — one for primary care, one for specialty visits, and a third for billing and lab results.

The result was predictable: patients with multiple care relationships maintained multiple logins. Information was duplicated, sometimes inconsistent. Scheduling friction drove a measurable share of appointments to phone-based booking, increasing call center load. The IT team spent the majority of its time integrating data across systems instead of building new capabilities.

Leadership had attempted a unified portal twice before. Both attempts stalled in the design phase — neither delivered to production. The third attempt needed a different approach.

The approach

How we did it.

We started where the previous attempts had stalled: not with the visual design or the technical architecture, but with the data model. The fundamental obstacle to a unified portal wasn't UX — it was that the underlying systems didn't agree on basic concepts like "patient identity," "appointment," and "encounter."

Phase 1: Identity & integration foundation (weeks 1–8)

We built a master patient index that resolved identities across all source systems, using a combination of deterministic matching (SSN, MRN, DOB) and probabilistic matching for ambiguous cases. The MPI sits in front of the three EHR systems and provides a single canonical patient ID to all downstream services.

On top of the MPI, we implemented a FHIR-native integration layer that exposed appointments, encounters, lab results, and clinical notes through standard FHIR resources. The previous portals had used three different ad-hoc APIs — we replaced them with one.

Phase 2: Portal & AI triage (weeks 9–18)

The unified portal was built as a React + Next.js application, deployed to a HIPAA-compliant AWS environment with Cognito for authentication. Patients now log in once and see appointments, results, billing, and messaging across all their care relationships.

The AI triage layer was the trickiest design decision. Most clinical AI tools force an "AI handles it" or "human handles it" choice. We built a hybrid system: an LLM-based intake agent gathers symptoms and context from the patient, classifies urgency (with explicit conservative bias), and routes to either a nurse triage queue or a scheduled visit. Crucially, the AI never closes a case unilaterally — every interaction ends with a human-reviewed handoff.

Phase 3: Migration & rollout (weeks 19–22)

We migrated 1.8 million active patient accounts across the three legacy portals through a coordinated cut-over weekend. Read-only access to the old portals remained available for 90 days, with automatic redirects guiding patients to the new system. The transition completed without a single missed appointment or lost message.

The outcome

What Atlas Health has now.

Beyond the headline metrics, the engagement delivered three durable capabilities:

A master patient index that other initiatives can rely on. The MPI has since become the foundation for a population health platform, a referrals management tool, and an upcoming patient-app strategy. The team no longer rebuilds identity matching for every new project.

A FHIR-native integration layer. Third-party vendors and partner systems integrate against modern FHIR endpoints instead of bespoke APIs. New integrations now take days instead of months.

An internal pattern for clinical AI deployment. The conservative-bias-with-human-handoff pattern from the AI triage is now used as a template for other clinical AI projects across the network — including a recently-launched provider-side documentation assistant.
