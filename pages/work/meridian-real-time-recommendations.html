Client Story · Retail · 18 weeks · 4 phases

A clickstream graveyard becomes a real-time revenue engine.

A national retail brand was sitting on years of clickstream data in cold storage. We built a Kafka-to-feature-store-to-recommendation pipeline that ships personalized experiences across every digital touchpoint — generating $24M in incremental annual revenue.

Client
Meridian Retail Group
Industry
Retail
Services
Big Data, AI/ML, Real-time Engineering
Timeline
18 weeks · 4 phases
The challenge

Data that wasn't earning its keep.

Meridian Retail Group operates 340 stores and a digital channel that drives roughly 35% of total revenue. The company had invested heavily in analytics infrastructure over the previous five years — Snowflake warehouse, dbt transformations, Looker for BI. The infrastructure worked. The data flowed. Dashboards were impressive.

But the data wasn't driving operational decisions. Recommendation systems on the website ran on three-year-old rules. Email campaigns segmented customers using static cohorts. The product team had been promising "personalization" for two years without shipping any.

The CDO had a clear mandate: turn the clickstream into operational decisions, in real time, with measurable revenue impact. Two prior attempts by internal teams had stalled — the real-time engineering proved harder than expected, and the team kept getting pulled into reporting work.

The approach

How we did it.

We started by ruthlessly scoping. There were 47 potential personalization use cases on the team's wish list. We picked three: product recommendations on the product detail page, search ranking, and the "for you" email subject line. Each had clear baseline metrics, measurable lift potential, and shared infrastructure requirements.

Phase 1: Real-time event pipeline (weeks 1–5)

We built a Kafka-based clickstream ingestion pipeline that captured 47 event types across web, mobile, and in-store touchpoints. Events flow through Kafka into a feature store (Tecton), where they're transformed into real-time features for model serving. The pipeline targets sub-second latency from event to feature availability.

Phase 2: Feature store & model training (weeks 6–10)

We built 184 features in the feature store covering session behavior, historical purchase patterns, product affinities, browse-to-buy ratios, and seasonal trends. Three models were trained and validated: a recommendation model (deep learning, two-tower architecture), a search ranking model (gradient-boosted with learn-to-rank loss), and a propensity model for email subject line selection.

Phase 3: Production deployment (weeks 11–15)

Models were served via a low-latency inference layer (FastAPI + Redis cache) targeting sub-50ms P95 latency. A/B testing infrastructure was built into the inference layer itself — every recommendation request can be split between treatment variants and control, with clean attribution.

Phase 4: Rollout & iteration (weeks 16–18)

We launched to 5% of traffic, then 25%, then 100% over three weeks. Every step was gated by measurable lift on the primary business metrics (conversion rate, AOV, email open rate). By week 18, all three use cases were running at 100% of relevant traffic with statistically significant lift.

The outcome

What Meridian has now.

The three launched use cases are themselves significant. But the more durable outcome is the platform underneath them.

A reusable real-time ML platform. The Kafka + Feature Store + inference layer architecture supports new use cases in days rather than months. The team has since launched six additional personalization features on the same infrastructure.

A working A/B testing discipline. Every personalization change ships through the A/B framework. The team now has 18 months of clean experimental data — including evidence of three features that didn't work, which they killed quickly and quietly.

Internal ML engineers who can operate it. Four Meridian engineers worked side-by-side with us throughout the build. They now lead the platform team and have hired two more engineers underneath them.
