Client Story · Manufacturing · 6 months · 23 plants

Predictive maintenance deployed across 23 plants.

A global industrial OEM was losing millions per quarter to unplanned downtime across 23 plants. A sensor-to-prediction-to-work-order pipeline deployed in waves over 6 months delivered measurable downtime reduction at every site.

Quantum+ Industrial
Industry

The challenge

Historian data with no operational impact.

Quantum+ Industrial manufactures heavy industrial equipment at 23 plants across North America and Europe. Each plant generates billions of sensor readings annually from PLCs, SCADA systems, and historians. The company had invested tens of millions over the previous decade in data collection — but the data sat in plant-local historians, rarely accessed beyond compliance reporting.

Unplanned downtime was costing roughly $48 million annually across the fleet. Maintenance teams ran on a fixed schedule (every X operating hours) regardless of actual equipment condition. Some machines were being serviced too early, wasting maintenance budget; others were failing between scheduled services, causing line-stopping incidents.

Two prior pilots had attempted predictive maintenance at single plants. Both showed promising results in proof-of-concept but never scaled. The integration with operational systems was too brittle. The maintenance teams didn't trust the predictions. The data science work didn't survive the trip from notebook to plant floor.

The approach

How we delivered.

We started by acknowledging the obvious lesson from the previous pilots: the model wasn't the hard part. The hard part was getting predictions into the hands of maintenance technicians, in their existing workflow, with enough trust that they'd act on them.

Phase 1: Data foundation at the first plant (weeks 1–8)

We started at a single representative plant. Sensor data flowed from PLC and historian into a cloud-based lakehouse (Databricks on Azure). Twelve months of historical data was backfilled to support model training. Real-time streaming gave us sub-minute end-to-end latency from sensor reading to feature availability.

Phase 2: Model development & validation (weeks 9–14)

We built three model families: anomaly detection (autoencoder-based) for unusual operating patterns, remaining useful life (RUL) prediction (survival analysis + LSTM) for failure forecasting, and root cause analysis (gradient-boosted decision trees) for explaining why a prediction was being made.

The explainability piece mattered as much as the accuracy. Maintenance technicians wouldn't act on a prediction they couldn't understand. Every prediction was paired with a plain-language reason ("vibration on bearing 3 has trended upward 18% over the past 72 hours, similar to the pattern seen before failure on March 4").

Phase 3: Operational integration at the first plant (weeks 15–22)

Predictions were integrated into the existing CMMS (computerized maintenance management system). When a prediction crossed the action threshold, a work order was created automatically — appearing in the technician's normal work queue, not in a separate "AI" interface.

Crucially, technicians could provide feedback on every prediction (accept, dispute, defer with reason). This feedback flowed back into the training loop, allowing the model to learn from operational reality rather than just lab data.

Phase 4: Rollout to 22 more plants (months 4–6)

With the pattern proven at the first plant, we rolled out to the remaining 22 plants in waves of 3-4 plants per month. Each rollout required local data pipeline configuration, model fine-tuning for site-specific equipment, and technician training. By the end of month 6, all 23 plants were live.

The outcome

What Quantum+ has now.

The platform is operational across all 23 plants and continues to deliver downtime reduction.

A reusable industrial AI platform. The lakehouse, feature engineering, and model serving infrastructure has been extended to two adjacent use cases: quality inspection (computer vision on the production line) and energy optimization. The platform is becoming the backbone of Quantum+'s broader Industry 4.0 strategy.

A maintenance team that uses the predictions. Adoption metrics tell a clearer story than the savings. Within six months of each plant going live, technician engagement with predictive work orders reached 89% (defined as predictions reviewed and acted on within 48 hours).

A culture of data-driven maintenance decisions. The maintenance leadership now reviews fleet-level dashboards in their weekly planning meetings, comparing plants and identifying which sites need additional investment. Decisions that used to be intuitive are now data-informed.
